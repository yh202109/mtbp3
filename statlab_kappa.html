StatLab/Cohen’s Kappa for inter-rater reliability

Background

Cohen’s kappa is a statistic used for describing, summarizing, estimating and testing inter-ratter consistency.

Notation

The observed raw percentage of agreement is defined as

\[p_O = \sum_{j=1}^J N_{jj} / N_{\bullet\bullet}\]

where \(J \geq 2\) is the size of value set.

Assume that

\[(N_{1\bullet}, \ldots N_{J\bullet}) \sim multi(N_{\bullet \bullet}, (p_{r=1,1}, \ldots, p_{r=1,J})),\]

and

\[(N_{\bullet 1}, \ldots N_{\bullet J}) \sim multi(N_{\bullet \bullet}, (p_{r=2,1}, \ldots, p_{r=2,J})),\]

with \(\sum N_{j \bullet} = \sum N_{\bullet j} = N_{\bullet \bullet}\) and \(\sum p_{r=1,j} = \sum p_{r=2, j} = 1\).

Under independence assumption, the expected number of agreement is estimated by \(\sum_{j=1}^J\hat{E}_{j} = \frac{1}{N_{\bullet \bullet}}\sum_{j=1}^J N_{\bullet j} N_{j\bullet} \equiv N_{\bullet \bullet}p_E\).

The \(\kappa\) statistic is calculated as

\[\kappa = \frac{p_O - p_E}{1-p_E}.\]

The SE of \(\kappa\) is calculated as

\[\sqrt{\frac{p_O(1-p_O)}{N_{\bullet \bullet}(1-p_E)^2}}.\]

## Interpretation in Literature

Cohen (1960)[1]_ suggested the Kappa result be interpreted as follows:

Kappa Interpretation

Value of \(\kappa\)

Interpretation

\(-1 \leq \kappa \leq 0\)

indicating no agreement

\($0 < \kappa \leq 0.2\)

none to slight

\(0.2 < \kappa \leq 0.4\)

fair

\(0.4 < \kappa \leq 0.6\)

moderate

\($0.6 < \kappa \leq 0.8\)

substantial

\(0.8 < \kappa \leq 1\)

almost perfect agreement
